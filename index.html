About Us

Welcome to the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center, your gateway to nature in the heart of Quezon City!

Managed by the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR), the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center (NAPWC) is a 23.85-hectare ecological sanctuary that serves as a haven for Philippine flora and fauna. Established in 1954, it is named in honour of the late Senator Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., reflecting his advocacy for environmental preservation.

Located just a stone’s throw away from Metro Manila's bustling urban life, the park offers a serene retreat where visitors can reconnect with nature. The center is home to diverse ecosystems, including a man-made lagoon, tree-lined paths, and open green spaces that showcase the country’s rich biodiversity.

The NAPWC is more than just a park—it is a commitment to preserving the natural heritage of the Philippines. Whether you're a nature lover, a conservation enthusiast, or simply seeking a peaceful escape, the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center invites you to explore, learn, and contribute to the conservation of our environment.
